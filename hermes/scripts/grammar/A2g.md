## A2 Grammar

- Nouns & Cases
  - Mastery of all six cases in singular and plural forms
  - Possibly intro genitive, dative, and instrumental if not covered in A1
  - Freely decline nouns and choose correct case endings
- Pronouns
  - Declension of personal pronouns in all cases
  - Use of demonstrative pronouns in various cases
  - Use of possessive pronouns in various cases
  - Introducing reflexive pronoun себя in set phrases
  - кто-то и что-то indefininte pronouns
- Adjectives
  - Full adjective declension in singular and plural across cases
  - Agreement of adjectives with nouns in complex phrases
  - Comparative degree of adjectives to make simple comparisons (adding -ее or using более for longer adjectives)
  - Forming superlatives with самый
- Numerals
  - Extended use of numbers, including hundreds and thousands
  - Grammatical agreement of numerals with nouns
  - Understanding the cases after 1, 2-4, and 5+
  - Using ordinal numbers in dates and ordered lists
- Verbs
  - Confident use of present, past, and future tenses with both imperfective and perfective verbs
  - Perfective verbs introduced?
  - Learnes can choose correct aspect in context
- Verb moods
  - Broader use of the imperative mood (incl. negatives)
  - Introduction of the conditional mood (бы + past tense) for simple hypotheticals
- Modals and infinitives
  - Common modal expressions and verbs (мочь, должен, нужно, можно, нельзя) with dative
  - Infinitive constructions (хочу сказать) and modal combination (должен сказать)
- Verbs of motion
  - Deeper understanding of base motion verbs
  - Differentiate uni vs muldirectional usage in various tenses
  - Intro of common prefixed motion verbs to express arrival/departure and the prepositions к и от
- Complex sentences
  - Subordinate clauses frequently used
  - Learners can use relative pronoun который to form relative clauses
  - Causal clauses with потому что and поэтому
  - Time clauses with когда and когда-то
  - Simple indirect speech to report info (Он сказал, что...)
- Negation
  - Proper use of double negation
  - Negative pronouns and adverbs are used to express absence of people/things/actions (никто, ничего, нигде, никогда)
- Other
  - Growing use of impersonal sentences and impersonal constructions with dative
  - Basic use of reflexive verbs for common actions and passive-like meanings
